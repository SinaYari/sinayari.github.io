About

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at George Mason University, working with Professor Hakan Aydin. My research centers on safety-critical real-time embedded systems, with a strong focus on mitigating schedule-based side-channel attacks and designing secure systems through system-level scheduling. My work investigates how scheduling decisions in real-time systems can unintentionally leak sensitive information, and how to systematically eliminate these vulnerabilities. I develop security-aware scheduling techniques—including task splitting, priority assignment strategies, run-time delay injection, and DAG-based scheduling—to prevent information leakage while preserving schedulability, timing guarantees, and system performance. More broadly, I am interested in building secure and dependable embedded systems that operate under strict timing and safety constraints. My goal is to design systems that are not only efficient and reliable but also resilient against emerging system-level threats. In addition to my research, I have a strong interest in software engineering, system design, and programming, particularly in domains involving automation, safety-critical applications, and high-assurance systems. I am actively seeking opportunities where I can apply my expertise to develop robust, secure, and scalable systems in real-world environments.
