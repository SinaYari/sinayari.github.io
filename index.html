About

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at George Mason University, working with Professor Hakan Aydin. My research focuses on secure and efficient real-time embedded systems, with a particular emphasis on mitigating schedule-based side-channel attacks in safety-critical systems.

My work explores how scheduling decisions in real-time systems can unintentionally leak sensitive information, and how to design security-aware scheduling techniques to prevent such attacks. I have developed methods, including task splitting, priority assignment strategies, run-time delay injection, and DAG-based scheduling, to eliminate different types of schedule-based attacks while preserving system schedulability and performance.

More broadly, I am interested in safety-critical embedded systems, where I design techniques that balance security, timing guarantees, and resource efficiency. My goal is to build systems that are not only high-performance and reliable, but also resilient against emerging system-level attacks.
